# California Legal Practice Reference

## California Court Hierarchy

```
California Supreme Court
  7 justices. Final authority on California law.
  Binding on ALL California courts.
           |
California Courts of Appeal (6 Districts)
  1st District: San Francisco (Divs. 1-4)
  2nd District: Los Angeles (Divs. 1-8)
  3rd District: Sacramento
  4th District: San Diego (Divs. 1-3)
  5th District: Fresno
  6th District: San Jose
  106 justices total. Decide in 3-justice panels.
           |
Superior Courts (Trial Courts)
  58 counties, one per county.
  No precedential value.
```

## Binding vs. Persuasive Authority in California

| Authority | Binding On | Notes |
|-----------|-----------|-------|
| CA Supreme Court | All CA courts | Always binding on state law questions |
| Published Court of Appeal (any district) | All Superior Courts statewide | A 1st District opinion binds a 4th District Superior Court |
| Published Court of Appeal (same district) | Other divisions in the same district follow as a matter of practice, but not technically bound | |
| Published Court of Appeal (different district) | NOT binding on other districts' Courts of Appeal | When districts conflict, trial courts choose; Supreme Court may resolve |
| Unpublished Court of Appeal | NOBODY — cannot be cited | Narrow exceptions below |
| U.S. Supreme Court | All courts on federal constitutional questions | |
| Ninth Circuit | Federal courts in the circuit; persuasive (not binding) in CA state courts | |
| Other federal circuits | Persuasive only | |
| Other state supreme courts | Persuasive only | |
| Trial court opinions | Nobody | No precedential value |

## Published vs. Unpublished Opinions

### California Rules of Court 8.1115

**General rule**: Unpublished Court of Appeal or superior court appellate division opinions **must not be cited or relied on** by any court or party.

**Exceptions** (the only times you may cite an unpublished opinion):
1. **Law of the case, res judicata, or collateral estoppel**: When the unpublished opinion is relevant under these doctrines
2. **Criminal or disciplinary actions**: When the opinion states reasons for a decision affecting **the same defendant or respondent** in another such action

**If citing under an exception**: Attach copies of the unpublished opinion to the brief and serve on all parties.

### Publication and Depublication

- Court of Appeal opinions are **unpublished by default**
- Published only if **certified for publication** by the authoring court or **ordered published** by the Supreme Court
- Published opinions are citable immediately upon certification
- **When Supreme Court grants review**: The Court of Appeal opinion's precedential effect is **suspended**. It may be cited only for "potentially persuasive value" and must note the grant of review.
- **Depublication**: The Supreme Court can order a published opinion depublished — removing precedential value without reviewing the merits. Unique to California.

## Citation Formats

California Rule of Court 1.200 permits either the **California Style Manual (CSM)** or the **Bluebook**, provided one is used consistently throughout.

### California Style Manual (preferred by CA courts)

**Cases**:
```
People v. Smith (2020) 10 Cal.5th 100, 105.
People v. Jones (2023) 95 Cal.App.5th 450, 460.
```
- Year comes BEFORE volume/reporter
- Use official CA reporters: Cal.5th, Cal.App.5th
- Pinpoint cite to specific page

**Subsequent references** (in later paragraphs):
```
Smith, supra, 10 Cal.5th at p. 107.
```

**Statutes**:
```
Pen. Code, § 745, subd. (a).
Evid. Code, § 1043.
Gov. Code, § 3301.
```
- Spell out "subdivision" (not "sub." or just "(a)")

### Bluebook (standard for federal courts and law reviews)

**Cases**:
```
People v. Smith, 470 P.3d 500, 503 (Cal. 2020).
People v. Jones, 310 Cal. Rptr. 3d 100, 110 (Ct. App. 2023).
```
- Year comes AFTER in parenthetical
- Use West reporters: P.3d, Cal. Rptr. 3d

**Statutes**:
```
Cal. Penal Code § 745(a) (West 2021).
```

### When to Use Which

| Context | Format |
|---------|--------|
| California state court filings | CSM (preferred) or Bluebook |
| California appellate briefs | CSM strongly recommended |
| Federal court filings (including 9th Circuit) | Bluebook |
| Law review articles | Bluebook |
| Internal memoranda | Follow office convention |
